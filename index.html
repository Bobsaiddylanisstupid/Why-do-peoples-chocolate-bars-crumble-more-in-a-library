Why Chocolate Bars Crumble More In Libraries

A highly serious scientific investigation conducted by Arthur, who accidentally destroyed an entire chocolate bar in the silent section.

Arthur's Discovery

One quiet afternoon, Arthur entered a library carrying a perfectly solid chocolate bar. Moments later, while opening it beside the history books, the bar exploded into crumbs.


Since that tragic event, Arthur has dedicated his life to discovering why chocolate becomes dramatically more fragile inside libraries.

The Main Theories

Extreme Silence

Every tiny crunch sounds 50 times louder in a library, making normal crumbs feel catastrophic.

Book Dust

Arthur believes ancient book dust weakens chocolate structure through mysterious crumb vibrations.

Nervous Hands

People squeeze chocolate too tightly because they fear making noise, accidentally crushing it.

Library Physics

According to Arthur, libraries operate under special snack-related physics that scientists refuse to study.

Arthur's Official Advice

  • Never eat crunchy chocolate near librarians.
  • Open wrappers slowly and carefully.
  • Always carry emergency crumb napkins.
  • Do not panic if crumbs reach the carpet.
